Chapter Two

The Twists

1

Confession: I was supposed to become an Electrical Engineer. Circuits, current, the whole responsible-adult career plan. Then I realized I was far more curious about why someone stops mid-scroll than why electrons move — so somewhere between wiring diagrams and Instagram reels, I quietly changed careers (without telling anyone).

2

Since then, I've built brand worlds from actual scratch: The Khione Store's quiet, earthy elegance; Wiggly Eets' panda-powered chaos energy. I took one client's reel views from 200 to 22,000 (not a typo) and roughly tripled their monthly inquiries — less magic, more paying uncomfortably close attention to what people actually respond to.

3

Plot twist number two: I didn't just wander into an MBA — I went looking for one, because I got genuinely obsessed with what makes people tick. Turns out "I have a good eye" only gets you so far, so I studied marketing properly — the kind that keeps clients actually happy — and analytics, so I could pull the best out of data instead of guessing and hoping. SQL, Python, Power BI, a banking research project, some dashboard-building along the way.

4

Oh, and before any of this — I ran a ₹24 lakh infrastructure project as a Project Manager. So budgets, deadlines, and five stakeholders pulling in five directions? Already survived that.

Bottom line: if your brand idea is still living in your Notes app, half-formed and a little embarrassing — that's exactly where I come in.
